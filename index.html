Biography

A short academic introduction

Hi! I am Yue Wu, currently pursuing my M.S. degree in Computer Technology at Huazhong University of Science and Technology. Before that, I received interdisciplinary training at Shanghai Jiao Tong University, where I studied Naval Architecture and Ocean Engineering together with Mathematics and Applied Mathematics.

My current research lies at the intersection of 3D generation, video generation, and world models. Recently, I have been working on 3D memory-augmented autoregressive video generation, where persistent spatial memory is used to improve long-term consistency in dynamic world simulation. Feel free to contact me for discussion !

AeroGen

AeroGen: JSON-Conditioned 3D Aircraft Generation

NeurIPS 2026, under review

Yue Wu, et al.

We study end-to-end controllable 3D aircraft generation from structured JSON parameters. The method represents heterogeneous aircraft components, spatial configurations, global scale, and auxiliary attributes to improve controllability and geometric precision.

Selected Projects

Research directions and ongoing work
3D Memory

3D Memory-Augmented Autoregressive Video Generation

Ongoing research, 2026–Present

This project studies how persistent 3D memory can be integrated into autoregressive video generation to reduce drift, improve spatial-temporal consistency, and support interactive world simulation. I am particularly interested in memory representations such as surfel-indexed memory and state-aware retrieval.

World Models Video Generation 3D Memory Long-Term Consistency
SceneDINO

Geometry Priors for 3D Semantic Scene Understanding

Research exploration, 2026

I explored how frozen geometric predictors, depth priors, and VGGT-style 3D cues can assist SceneDINO-like feature fields for semantic scene completion on KITTI-360 SSCBench. The goal is to understand how semantic features and geometric priors can help each other in 3D space.

SceneDINO VGGT Depth Prior KITTI-360
AeroGen

AeroGen: JSON-Conditioned 3D Aircraft Generation

Master research

This project focuses on generating 3D aircraft models from structured design descriptions. Compared with purely image-conditioned generation, structured inputs provide fine-grained control over part topology, aircraft geometry, and engineering-related design attributes.
